Famous Local Cuisine of Jharkhand

Thekua

Discription:A sweet and crispy snack made from wheat flour, jaggery, and coconut, Thekua is especially popular during the Chhath Puja festival.

Estimated Cost: ₹50 - ₹100 per packet

Litti-Chokha

Discription: While also a signature of Bihar, this dish is very popular in Jharkhand. Litti are baked or roasted dough balls stuffed with a spiced mixture of roasted gram flour (sattu). They are typically served with "chokha," a mashed mix of roasted eggplant, tomatoes, and potatoes.

Estimated Cost: ₹70 - ₹150 per serving

Dhuska

Discription:This is one of the most popular breakfast items in Jharkhand. It's a deep-fried bread made from a batter of rice and lentils (chana dal and urad dal), often served with a spicy potato curry or a chickpea stew called "ghugni."

Estimated Cost: ₹40 - ₹80 per serving

Chilka Roti

Discription: This is a thin, pancake-like flatbread made from a batter of rice flour and lentils. It's often served with a vegetable curry, mutton curry, or a simple chutney.

Estimated Cost: ₹30 - ₹60 per serving

Handia (Rice Beer)

Discription: A traditional rice beer brewed by local communities. It's made by fermenting a mix of boiled rice and a special "ranu" tablet made from various herbs. It has a low alcohol content.

Estimated Cost: ₹20 - ₹50 per glass

Bamboo Shoot Curry

Discription:A tribal delicacy, this curry is made from tender bamboo shoots cooked with mustard seeds, garlic, and green chilies.

Estimated Cost: ₹50 - ₹100 per Kg

Rugra Mushroom

Discription:This is a type of edible mushroom that grows in Jharkhand, especially during the monsoon season. It's considered a delicacy and is used to make a flavorful curry.

Estimated Cost: ₹500 - ₹800 per Kg

Pitha

Discription:These are dumplings made from rice flour, with both sweet and savory versions. The sweet ones are often filled with jaggery and coconut, while savory ones might contain vegetables or lentils. They can be steamed or pan-fried.

Estimated Cost: ₹10 - ₹20 per piece
