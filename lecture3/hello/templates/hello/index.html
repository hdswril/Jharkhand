About Jharkhand

Jharkhand, meaning "The Land of Forests," is a state rich in mineral resources, breathtaking waterfalls, ancient temples, and vibrant tribal culture.

Natural Beauty

Home to numerous waterfalls, hills, and dense forests including the famous Betla National Park.

Tribal Heritage

Rich cultural heritage with 32 indigenous tribes preserving ancient traditions and art forms.

Mineral Wealth

Known as India's mineral heartland with abundant resources of coal, iron, and other minerals.

Popular Destinations

Explore the most breathtaking places in Jharkhand

Dassam Falls

A magnificent waterfall where the Kanchi River falls from a height of 144 feet.

Betla National Park

Home to tigers, elephants, and various wildlife species in their natural habitat.

Jubilee Park

A beautifully landscaped park in Jamshedpur with fountains and recreational facilities.

Hundru Falls

One of the highest waterfalls in Jharkhand, creating a spectacular sight during monsoon.

Baidyanath Temple

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas, a significant pilgrimage site for Hindus.

Netarhat

Known as the 'Queen of Chotanagpur,' offering breathtaking views and pleasant climate.

Cultural Heritage

Experience the rich tribal culture and traditions of Jharkhand

Tribal Art & Craft

Jharkhand is home to exquisite tribal art forms including:

  • Paitkar Painting - Scroll paintings depicting tribal life
  • Sohrai and Khovar Art - Traditional wall paintings
  • Traditional Jewelry - Dokra metal craft and bead work
  • Handloom Textiles - Tussar silk and tribal patterns

Vibrant Festivals

Celebrate the colorful festivals of Jharkhand

Sarhul

Spring festival worshiping the Sal tree and nature deities.

Karma

Harvest festival celebrated with traditional dance and music.

Tusu Parab

Winter harvest festival celebrated by tribal communities.

Holi

Festival of colors celebrated with unique local traditions.
