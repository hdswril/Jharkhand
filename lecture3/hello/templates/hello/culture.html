Cultural Arts and Crafts of Jharkhand

Explore the rich heritage, traditions, and artistry of Jharkhand

Sohrai Paintings

Description: Sohrai is a harvest festival celebrated by tribal communities. The paintings are made using natural pigments on mud walls, depicting animals, trees, and rural life symbolizing prosperity.

Origin: Hazaribagh district and other tribal regions of Jharkhand.

Estimated Price: ₹600–₹3,000 depending on size and intricacy.

Khovar Paintings

Description: Khovar paintings are created during weddings with geometric patterns and motifs like peacocks and flowers, symbolizing fertility and happiness.

Origin: Hazaribagh and Palamu districts.

Estimated Price: ₹1,000–₹5,000 depending on complexity.

Paitkar Paintings

Description: Scroll paintings narrating mythological or historical stories through vivid illustrations, often accompanied by songs.

Origin: Amadubi village of Jharkhand.

Estimated Price: ₹700–₹2,500 depending on length and detail.

Jadopatia Paintings

Description: Optical illusion art where designs change appearance from different angles through intricate patterns and colors.

Origin: Dumka district.

Estimated Price: ₹1,500–₹4,000 depending on design complexity.

Dhokra Craft

Description: Ancient metal casting technique using lost-wax method to create brass figurines, jewelry, and utensils.

Origin: Khunti and Sikaripara regions.

Estimated Price: ₹500–₹5,000 depending on size and intricacy.

Bamboo and Cane Crafts

Description: Woven baskets, mats, and utility items made from bamboo and cane, lightweight and eco-friendly.

Origin: Ranchi and Khunti districts.

Estimated Price: ₹100–₹1,500 depending on item and craftsmanship.

Tribal Jewelry

Description: Bold designs using beads, metals, and stones, often representing cultural identity and status.

Origin: Ranchi and Khunti regions.

Estimated Price: ₹200–₹2,000 depending on material and design complexity.

Chhau Masks

Description: Papier-mâché masks painted vividly for Chhau dance, representing deities and mythological characters.

Origin: Seraikela and Kharsawan regions.

Estimated Price: ₹1,000–₹3,000 depending on size and detailing.

Wooden Handicrafts

Description: Carved wooden toys, utensils, and decorative pieces with traditional motifs sourced from local forests.

Origin: Ranchi and Chaibasa districts.

Estimated Price: ₹150–₹2,000 depending on size and craftsmanship.

Terracotta Art

Description: Clay molded items like masks, pots, and figurines fired in kilns and painted with natural dyes.

Origin: Dumka and Deoghar districts.

Estimated Price: ₹200–₹3,000 depending on item and design.
